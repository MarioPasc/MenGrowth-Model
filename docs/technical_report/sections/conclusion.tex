\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
% 10.1 Summary of Contributions
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Summary of Contributions}
\label{sec:conc:summary}

This thesis has presented a four-phase pipeline for meningioma growth
forecasting from longitudinal multi-modal 3D MRI. The key contributions and
findings are:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Foundation model adaptation.} We demonstrated that
    BrainSegFounder, pretrained on 41,400+ subjects for glioma segmentation,
    can be adapted to the meningioma domain via LoRA with $\sim$197K
    trainable parameters ($<0.32\%$ of total). However, the marginal
    improvement in downstream feature quality ($\Delta R^2 = +0.016$)
    suggests that SSL pretraining already provides strong cross-domain
    features, and the primary benefit of LoRA is in segmentation quality
    rather than representation structure.

  \item \textbf{Supervised disentangled projection.} We designed a
    non-generative approach to structured latent space construction,
    theoretically grounded in the impossibility of unsupervised
    disentanglement \cite{locatello2019challenging} and validated against
    the failure of VAE-based alternatives. The combination of semantic
    regression, VICReg regularisation, and distance correlation provides a
    principled and computationally efficient framework.

  \item \textbf{Empirically-driven methodology revision.} Rigorous
    evaluation revealed that shape disentanglement fails ($R^2 \leq 0.11$),
    meningioma location is temporally static, and sub-compartment volume
    targets are label-dependent and clinically irrelevant. These findings
    motivated a principled simplification from a $3+1$ partition SDP with
    PA-MOGP to a $1+1$ partition SDP with standard MOGP.

  \item \textbf{GP hierarchy for small-sample temporal modelling.} The
    three-model hierarchy (LME $\to$ H-GP $\to$ PA-MOGP) replaces the
    originally proposed Neural ODE, which was catastrophically
    overparameterised for 33~patients ($\sim$3,100 parameters vs.\ 112
    observation pairs). The GP framework provides closed-form inference,
    calibrated uncertainty, and principled handling of heterogeneous
    observation counts.

  \item \textbf{Negative results on dual-domain transfer.} Cross-domain
    GP probes yield $R^2 \approx -0.01$, demonstrating that domain gap
    reduction (MMD$^2$ decrease of 68.6\%) does not guarantee semantic
    compatibility. Dual-domain training compresses the effective rank by
    46.6\% while degrading MEN segmentation Dice from 0.64 to 0.36.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Limitations}
\label{sec:conc:limitations}

\subsubsection{Cohort Size}

The Andalusian longitudinal cohort (33~patients, 100~observations) is the
primary bottleneck. With 57.6\% of patients having only 2~studies, the
per-patient growth trajectories are sparse. While the GP framework handles
this gracefully via automatic shrinkage toward the population mean, the
resulting predictions for 2-observation patients are dominated by the
population trend rather than individual-specific dynamics.

\subsubsection{Temporal Sparsity}

The irregular and sparse temporal sampling (2--6 observations per patient
over months to years) limits the detection of non-monotonic growth patterns
(e.g., saltatory growth, temporary regression). The Mat\'{e}rn-5/2 kernel
imposes a smoothness prior that may be inappropriate for tumours with
abrupt growth regime changes.

\subsubsection{LoRA Marginality}

The finding that LoRA adaptation provides marginal downstream benefit
($\Delta R^2 = +0.016$) raises the question of whether the Phase~1
adaptation step is necessary at all for this pipeline. The frozen
BrainSegFounder encoder may suffice, with the SDP and GP models providing
the primary modelling value. However, LoRA does improve segmentation
quality, which affects the accuracy of the semantic feature extraction used
to train the SDP.

\subsubsection{Single-Site Longitudinal Data}

The Andalusian cohort is collected from hospitals in a single region, which
may limit generalisability to other populations, scanner manufacturers, and
acquisition protocols. ComBat harmonisation addresses scanner batch effects
but cannot correct for biological population differences.

\subsubsection{Absence of Clinical Validation}

The pipeline has not been validated against clinical outcomes (intervention
decisions, actual tumour behaviour at longer follow-up). The volume
prediction $R^2$ and calibration metrics are necessary but not sufficient
for clinical utility.

\subsection{Future Work}
\label{sec:conc:future}

\subsubsection{Larger Longitudinal Cohorts}

The most impactful improvement would be access to larger longitudinal
meningioma cohorts. Multi-centre collaborations could provide 100--500
patients, which would enable: (i)~proper training/validation/test splits
for GP models; (ii)~exploration of higher-capacity models (deep kernel
GPs, neural process families); (iii)~subgroup analysis by tumour grade,
location, and patient demographics.

\subsubsection{Self-Supervised Temporal Pretraining}

Following the JEPA paradigm \cite{lecun2022path}, the SDP and GP models
could be replaced by a self-supervised temporal prediction objective:
given the latent vector at $t_0$, predict the latent vector at $t_1$ in
representation space. This would require more longitudinal data but could
learn temporal dynamics end-to-end rather than relying on the two-stage
SDP + GP approach.

\subsubsection{Clinical Decision Support Integration}

The GP posterior's calibrated uncertainty is directly suitable for clinical
decision support: the probability that a tumour will exceed a volume
threshold $V_{\text{critical}}$ within a specified time horizon can be
computed analytically from the Gaussian predictive distribution. Integrating
this into a clinical workflow would require prospective validation and
regulatory approval.

\subsubsection{Multi-Task Growth Prediction}

Rather than predicting volume alone, the pipeline could be extended to
jointly predict clinical endpoints (e.g., symptom onset, need for surgery)
from the latent trajectories. This would require outcome labels that are
typically available in retrospective clinical databases.

\subsubsection{Foundation Model Scaling}

BrainSegFounder is a relatively small foundation model (BSF-Tiny, 62M
parameters). Larger variants or more recent foundation models pretrained on
broader datasets may provide stronger initial features, potentially
eliminating the need for LoRA adaptation entirely and improving the SDP's
semantic decodability.
